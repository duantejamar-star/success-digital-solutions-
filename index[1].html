What are typical project timelines for a penetration test? expand_more

The total timeline varies based on your network footprint or code complexity, but a standard cycle follows this structure:

Security Terminology

Cybersecurity Glossary

Defining key concepts to educate our clients, demystify risk metrics, and make security decisions accessible.

Zero-Trust

Core Model

A security framework based on the principle of "never trust, always verify." No user or device is trusted by default, whether inside or outside the network. Continuous verification is required at every step.

Business Impact & Value:

Ensures that even if an attacker compromises a single credential or device, they cannot freely access or damage the rest of your systems.

Penetration Testing

Offensive Auditing

An authorized, simulated attack on your systems, applications, or infrastructure. Our experts think and act like cybercriminals to find exploit paths before real threat actors do.

Business Impact & Value:

Reveals concrete security gaps and logical bypass opportunities before malicious actors can exploit them, saving your business from high breach-response costs.

Fuzzing

Automated Fuzz Testing

A testing method that automatically injects massive volumes of malformed, unexpected, or random payloads into target endpoints and monitors for failures or memory leaks.

Business Impact & Value:

Uncovers deep, edge-case logical defects, rate-limit failures, and stack-buffer overruns that manual code inspection would easily overlook.

Encryption

Data Protection

Converting sensitive plaintext records into mathematical ciphertext. Protected in transit (when crossing the network) and at rest (when saved on disk drives).

Business Impact & Value:

Guarantees complete information confidentiality; even in the event of hardware theft or database interception, the raw data remains completely unreadable.

OWASP Top 10

Industry Benchmark

An international security standard ranking the ten most critical and prevalent web application vulnerabilities, maintained by the Open Web Application Security Project.

Business Impact & Value:

Offers a verified framework for technical code audits, helping you confirm that your APIs and client services are guarded against industry-standard attacks.

Multi-Factor Auth (MFA)

Credential Security

A security protocol forcing users to supply multiple independent tokens to log in (typically: password + time-based token from an authenticator application).

Business Impact & Value:

Completely neutralizes simple credential compromises, ensuring that password leaks alone cannot expose internal administration channels.
